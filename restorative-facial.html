What is the Restorative Buccal Facial?

The Restorative Buccal Facial is a manual facial technique in which the practitioner wears sterile gloves and works both outside and inside the cheek to manipulate the muscles, fascia, and connective tissue of the face. The word "buccal" refers to the cheek, from the Latin bucca. Unlike a standard facial that works only on the skin's surface, the Restorative Buccal Facial accesses the interior surface of the cheek to reach the buccinator, masseter, and pterygoid muscles, which cannot be fully addressed from the outside.

At Altru Radiance in Murray, Utah, the Restorative Buccal Facial integrates intraoral fascia work with manual lymphatic drainage, fascia-informed facial work, and Korean skincare.

Where conventional skincare can't reach

Most facials operate on the skin's surface: cleansing, exfoliating, infusing serums. These have value, but they have a ceiling. Beneath the skin, the fascial network and the chronically held tension in the jaw and midface shape much of what we see as facial heaviness, asymmetry, and the appearance of aging.

Three layers, one access point.

The Restorative Buccal Facial works with the fascia of the midface, softens masseter and pterygoid tension through intraoral access, and supports cervical and facial lymphatic flow. The result is a visibly lifted appearance: jaw softening, more open contour through the lower face, and a face that sits the way it's meant to sit.

What's included in your Restorative Buccal Facial

  1. Intake & Skin Assessment

    Brief consultation about jaw tension, facial holding patterns, recent dental or injectable work, and any jaw tension or holding patterns. Mechelle reviews your goals and customizes the session to your tissue and history.

  2. Manual Lymphatic Drainage Prep

    External lymphatic work opens cervical drainage channels, calms the nervous system, and softens tissue before the deeper work begins. This stage on its own is what most clients describe as the most relaxing treatment they have experienced.

  3. Facial Fascia Integration

    External hands-on fascia work integrates with the rest of the face: brow line, cheekbones, jaw definition, and neck. This stage settles the surrounding tissue and supports a sense of symmetry, preparing the deeper layers for the intraoral work that follows.

  4. Intraoral Buccal Work

    With sterile gloves, Mechelle works inside the cheek with the buccinator, masseter, medial and lateral pterygoids, and the fascial planes connecting them. Pressure builds gradually as the tissue softens. This is the core of the session.

  5. Skincare Closeout

    Targeted serums and skincare products matched to your skin type and concerns. Red light therapy is available as an add-on for clients prioritizing collagen support and surface vitality.

How is the Restorative Buccal Facial different from a regular facial?

A regular facial works on the skin's surface: cleansing, exfoliating, applying actives. The Restorative Buccal Facial works on the fascia layer beneath. Sterile gloves allow the practitioner to work with facial muscles and fascia from inside the cheek, accessing the buccinator, masseter, and pterygoid muscles that cannot be reached externally. The goal is visible lift and a softer expression, not surface optimization.

For a deeper comparison, see our article on fascia-informed facial versus traditional facial.

Does the Restorative Buccal Facial hurt?

The Restorative Buccal Facial involves deliberate pressure on areas of held tension, which most clients describe as a profound sense of release rather than pain. The intraoral portion begins with light pressure and builds gradually as tissue softens. Areas of held masseter or pterygoid tension may feel tender, but the work is never meant to be painful. Mechelle adjusts pressure throughout based on your feedback.

Is the Restorative Buccal Facial safe?

The Restorative Buccal Facial performed by a trained, licensed esthetician using sterile gloves is a safe manual technique. Contraindications include active oral infections, cold sores, recent oral surgery, or being within two weeks of injectable treatments. Mechelle reviews your health history at intake and adjusts the session accordingly.

How does jaw tension show up in the face, and how does the Restorative Buccal Facial help?

Jaw tension affects how the face looks — a heavier lower face, a more set expression, deeper folds along the jaw line. The Restorative Buccal Facial's gentle intraoral work supports a softer, more lifted appearance in the mid to lower face. It's a cosmetic facial focused on beautification — what you see in the mirror is more lift, more softness, more you.

Is the Restorative Buccal Facial a natural alternative to muscle-targeted injections?

The Restorative Buccal Facial and muscle-targeted injection treatments work through different mechanisms. Injection treatments block muscle contraction; the Restorative Buccal Facial works with muscle tension and fascia at the surface. For jaw and masseter concerns, the Restorative Buccal Facial can support visible jaw softening through a different pathway, with no recovery time, and no ongoing chemical exposure. See our journal article on hands-first facial alternatives for the full comparison.

How long do Restorative Buccal Facial results last?

Visible lift and decongestion from a single session typically remain noticeable for one to two weeks. Visible change builds cumulatively across a series and is sustained through monthly maintenance. The Restorative Buccal Facial does not create permanent change: it softens held tension and supports the body's natural lift, which the muscles and fascia will revert toward without ongoing care.

What should I do to prepare for my first Restorative Buccal Facial session?

Avoid a heavy meal in the 1 to 2 hours before your session. Arrive with a clean face. Disclose any jaw tension or holding patterns, recent dental work, or recent cosmetic injections (wait at least two weeks after any injection treatment). Drink water before and after to support the lymphatic component of the work.
